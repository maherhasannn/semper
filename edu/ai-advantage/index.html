The Curriculum

This is not beginner content. We assume you're smart, motivated, and ready to do the work. Each week escalates in technical depth and real-world application.

Week 1
LLM Architecture Deep-Dive

Go beyond surface-level understanding. Explore transformer architecture, attention mechanisms, tokenization strategies, and why certain prompts work better than others at a technical level.

Hands-on: Analyze token distributions, measure context window utilization, and benchmark prompt strategies across different model architectures.

Claude API OpenAI API Tokenizers
Week 2
RAG & Vector Databases

Build retrieval-augmented generation systems from scratch. Understand embedding models, chunking strategies, hybrid search, and how to ground LLMs in your organization's proprietary data.

Project: Deploy a production RAG pipeline on your own documents with Pinecone/Weaviate and measure retrieval accuracy.

Pinecone LangChain Embeddings
Week 3
Agentic Workflows

Build autonomous AI agents that can plan, execute, and iterate. Understand tool use, function calling, multi-step reasoning, and how to orchestrate complex workflows without human intervention.

Project: Build an agent that monitors data sources, synthesizes insights, and takes autonomous action based on defined triggers.

Claude Tool Use LangGraph CrewAI
Week 4
Multi-Modal Systems

Integrate vision, audio, and text into unified AI workflows. Build systems that can process documents, analyze images, transcribe meetings, and synthesize insights across modalities.

Project: Build a document intelligence system that extracts structured data from PDFs, images, and handwritten notes.

Vision APIs Whisper Document AI
Week 5
Fine-Tuning & Custom Models

Train models on your organization's specific use cases. Understand when to fine-tune vs. prompt engineer, how to prepare training data, and how to evaluate model performance systematically.

Project: Fine-tune a model on your domain-specific data and benchmark it against base models on real tasks.

AI Security & Governance

Understand prompt injection, jailbreaking, data leakage, and how to build secure AI systems. Learn enterprise governance frameworks, compliance considerations, and risk mitigation strategies.

Ship AI systems that scale. Understand caching, rate limiting, fallback strategies, monitoring, and how to maintain AI systems in production with proper observability and cost management.

FAQs

Common questions about the AI Advantage program.

What's the time commitment?

Expect 8-10 hours per week: 2 hours of live sessions plus 6-8 hours of hands-on project work. The program is designed for working professionals, with sessions scheduled outside standard business hours.

Do I need coding experience?

Basic Python proficiency is required. You should be comfortable with variables, functions, loops, and working in a code editor. If you can write a simple script, you're ready. We'll provide resources to fill any gaps before Week 1.

What's the application process?

Submit your application with answers to our screening questions. We review applications within 48 hours. If accepted, you'll receive enrollment instructions and can secure your spot immediately. We limit cohorts to 12 participants to ensure hands-on attention.

Can I use this for my actual work projects?

Yes - that's the point. The capstone project is designed to solve a real problem from your work or career. Many participants build systems they deploy at their companies or use to land their next role.

What if I need to miss a session?

All sessions are recorded with full transcripts. However, we strongly encourage live attendance for the interactive components and peer collaboration. If you expect to miss more than 2 sessions, consider a future cohort.
